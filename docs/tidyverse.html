Chapter 3 Tidyverse

Let us first read in some data. For this, we will read in the nettle climate data from Bodo Winter’s book.

nettle <- read.csv('/Users/umit/materials/data/nettle_1999_climate.csv')

Next, let us turn our nettle dataset into a tibble. A tibble is a dataframe with some improved capabilities. All we have to do is to call the as.tibble on our nettle variable and assign it back to the nettle variable. Make sure your tidyverse is loaded by running library(tidyverse) first.

library(tidyverse)
nettle <- as_tibble(nettle)

Now, you can simply run nettle and it will show the head of your dataframe.
